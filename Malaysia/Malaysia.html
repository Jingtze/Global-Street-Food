Nasi Lemak

Nasi lemak is a traditional Malaysian dish and one of the most iconic street food in Malaysia. It's made by rice cooked in coconut milk and pandan leaves, and usually served with a spicy chili paste called "sambal", fired crispy anchovies called "ikan bilis", roasted peanuts called "kacang tanah", egg called "telur" and cucumber slices called "timun".

Nasi lemak is wrapped in banana leaves or served on a plate. Nowadays, nasi lemak also can add dishes like fried chicken, sambal squid or rendang. Nasi lemak is a common breakfast choice in Malaysia but it's also popular to enjoy it at any time of day.

Satay

Satay is a popular street food in Malaysia which made from grilled chicken, beef or lamb skewers marinated with spices. Satay is usually grilled over charcoal, giving the satay a smoky flavour and slightly charred edges.

Satay is often served with a tangy peanut sauce, cucumber, onions, and rice cake called ketupat. We can enjoy satay at night market, food stalls and food trucks.

Apam Balik

Apam Balik is a common street food in Malaysia. It's a thick, fluffy pancake typically filled with crushed peanuts, sugar, and corn kernels.

At roadside stalls or night markets, vendors pour batter into a wok and fry it until golden brown on one side, adding the fillings halfway through. Then, they fold the pancake in half, and the Apam Balik we are familiar with is now complete. Besides traditional flavours, there are also creative options like cheese, chocolate, and even durian, making it a favorite for people of all ages.

Cendol

Cendol is a traditional Malaysian dessert and a popular street food during hot weather. It's made with shaved ice, coconut milk, gula melaka, and pandan jelly noodles, creating a refreshing and sweet treat.

Cendol is commonly found in hawker centers, night markets, and roadside stalls, serving as a refreshing summer treat for people of all ages. The rich aroma of coconut milk, the unique flavour of gula melaka, and the chewiness of rice jelly create this unique Malaysian classic desert.

Char Kway Teow

Char Kway Teow is one of Malaysia's most famous fried noodles, particularly popular as a street food. It's made with flat rice noodles stir-fried over high heat with soy sauce, eggs, bean sprouts, chives, shrimp, and often cockles, giving it a slightly smoky "wok hei" flavour.

Commonly found in hawker centers and night markets, char kway teow is cooked in a wok over high heat and is known for its rich, savory, and slightly burnt smell, making it a must-try for tourists and locals alike.

Murtabak

Murtabak is a common street food in Malaysia, drawing influences from Middle Eastern and Indian cuisine. It's a pan-fried, flatbread-wrapped filling typically filled with seasoned minced meat, such as beef or chicken, onions, and eggs.

Fried to a golden crisp, the mudabak boasts a crispy exterior and a juicy filling. It's often enjoyed with curry and pickled onions. Murtabak is a popular dish at night markets and hawker stalls, it represents a prime example of Malaysia's fusion cuisine.

Popiah

Popiah is a common Malaysian snack, originating from the Fujian and Teochew culinary cultures. It's a thin, rolled pancake filled with a variety of fillings, typically including jicama (water spinach), carrots, bean sprouts, egg, and chopped peanuts.

Popiah has a light and complex texture, blending the sweetness of vegetables with the aroma of peanuts and sauces. Commonly found at hawker centers and night markets, popiah is a classic example of Malaysia's diverse culinary culture.

Lok Lok

Lok Lok is a popular Malaysian street food, often found at night markets, roadside stalls, and temple fairs. It's served on skewers filled with meat, seafood, vegetables, and tofu, which customers can either blanch in boiling water or deep-fry in hot oil.

Once cooked, the lok lok is served with a variety of sauces, from peanut sauce to sweet sauce to chili sauce. Lok Lok is often enjoyed as a late-night snack.

Keropok Lekor

Keropok lekor are a signature traditional snack from the Malaysian state of Terengganu. Made from a mixture of fish and tapioca starch, they are rolled into strips and then fried or boiled. Fried fish cakes are crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, while boiled ones are softer and chewier.

They are often served with chili sauce or sweet and spicy sauce, the fishy aroma complementing the flavors of the sauce. A common street food, keropok lekor are affordable and popular among people of all ages, making them a Malaysian delicacy.

Ramly Burger

The Ramly Burger is one of Malaysia's most iconic street foods, known as the "Malaysian burger." Launched by local brand Ramly in the 1980s, it quickly became a hit at roadside stalls and night markets.

Its unique twist lies in the beef or chicken patty that's first coated in egg, then topped with sauce, mayonnaise, and vegetables, then sandwiched between soft buns. Its juicy texture and rich flavor have made it a classic late-night snack and street food choice for Malaysians.
