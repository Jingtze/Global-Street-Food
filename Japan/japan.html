Japan Street Food

Discover authentic flavors of Japan, from crispy takoyaki to fluffy okonomiyaki.


Takoyaki

Crisp-outside, custardy-inside batter balls with octopus, finished with sauce, mayo, and bonito flakes.

Main ingredients: Octopus Batter Bonito flakes

Okonomiyaki

Savory cabbage pancake griddled and finished with tangy sauce, mayo, and seaweed flakes.

Main ingredients: Cabbage Pork belly
Okonomiyaki sauce

Photo: ume-y (CC BY 2.0), via Wikimedia Commons

Taiyaki

Fish-shaped pastry filled with red bean paste, custard, or chocolate.

Main ingredients: flour sugar egg milk

Yakisoba

Wok-tossed noodles with pork and cabbage in a sweet–savory sauce.

Main ingredients: Wheat noodles Pork Cabbage

Photo: Tokumeigakarinoaoshima (CC BY-SA 4.0), via Wikimedia Commons

Dango

Chewy rice dumplings on a skewer, often glazed with sweet soy (mitarashi).

Main ingredients: Rice flour

Ikayaki

Whole squid brushed with soy, grilled until smoky and tender.

Main ingredients: Squid Soy sauce

Photo: suri (CC BY-SA 2.0), via Wikimedia Commons

Kakigōri

Feathery shaved ice crowned with syrups, fruit, and sometimes condensed milk.

Main ingredients: Ice Condensed milk

Korokke

Panko-crumbed croquettes—usually mashed potato—fried to a golden crunch.

Main ingredients: Potato Panko

Photo: 円周率３パーセント ( CC BY-SA 4.0 ), via Wikimedia Commons

Yaki Tomorokoshi

Charred corn on the cob brushed with soy butter—sweet, smoky, irresistible.

Main ingredients: Corn Butter Soy sauce

Imagawayaki

Thick round pancake with molten sweet fillings like adzuki or custard.

Main ingredients: Adzuki beans Batter

Photo: Tomomarusan (CC BY 2.5), via Wikimedia Commons
